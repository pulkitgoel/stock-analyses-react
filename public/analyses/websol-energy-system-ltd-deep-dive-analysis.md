## 1. Business Overview

**Websol Energy System Limited** (founded 1990, headquartered in Falta, West Bengal) is a leading Indian manufacturer of **photovoltaic (PV) crystalline solar cells and modules**. The company is a vertically integrated solar manufacturer serving India's domestic solar market, primarily through **DCR (Domestic Content Requirement)** tenders from SECI, NTPC, state DISCOMs, and C&I customers.

### Core Business

- **Solar Cells:** Mono PERC cells (current generation) transitioning to TOPCon (by Feb 2027)
- **Solar Modules:** Finished solar panels sold under ALMM (Approved List of Models and Manufacturers) compliant framework
- **Key Technology:** Metal Injection Molding (MIM)? No — this is a **solar PV manufacturing** company, not MIM. The business is built around crystalline silicon solar cell production.

### Manufacturing Capacity

| Parameter | Current (FY26) | Post-Expansion (Target) |
|-----------|---------------|------------------------|
| **Solar Cell Capacity** | 1.2 GW (2 lines of 600 MW) | 5.2+ GW (after Phase 3) |
| **Module Capacity** | 550 MW | ~4.5 GW (after Phase 3) |
| **Cell Utilisation** | >90% | Target maintained |
| **Module Utilisation** | 74% (Q4FY26) | Improving |
| **Cell Efficiency (Mono PERC)** | ~23.35% | TOPCon targeting >24.5% |
| **Phase 2 Cell Line** | Commissioned Sep 2025 | Operating at 85%+ utilisation |
| **TOPCon Upgrade** | Brownfield upgrade (₹250-270 Cr) | Commercial production Feb 2027 |
| **Phase 3 Greenfield** | 4 GW integrated (₹2,800-3,000 Cr) | June 2027 target |
| **Backward Integration** | MOU with Linton (ingot/wafer) | ALMM-3 deadline June 2028 |

### Key Competitive Advantages

1. ✅ **ALMM Policy Moat** — Domestic Content Requirement mandates protect Indian solar manufacturers from cheaper Chinese imports (15-20% premium pricing)
2. ✅ **Capacity Doubled in FY26** — From 600 MW to 1.2 GW cell capacity; operating at >90% utilisation
3. ✅ **Exceptional Return Ratios** — ROCE 63.2%, ROE 66.9% — among the highest in Indian manufacturing
4. ✅ **Strong Balance Sheet** — Net debt of only ₹92 Cr; Debt/Equity 0.21; Near net cash
5. ✅ **Full Order Book** — ₹1,161 Cr order book (~12 months revenue visibility)
6. ✅ **Technology Upgrade in Progress** — TOPCon upgrade targeting >24.5% cell efficiency
7. ✅ **Phase 3 Expansion** — 4 GW integrated facility in Andhra Pradesh (backed by state government incentives)

## 2. Industry Overview — Indian Solar Manufacturing

### Market Structure

- **Global solar PV market** dominated by China (>80% module production globally)
- **India's ALMM policy** mandates use of approved domestic modules for government/subsidised solar projects
- **DCR (Domestic Content Requirement)** tenders from SECI, NTPC, state DISCOMs create captive demand for Indian manufacturers
- **PM-KUSUM 2.0** and **PM Surya Ghar** schemes driving additional DCR solar demand

### Competitive Dynamics

| Player | Solar Cell Capacity | Key Advantage |
|--------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Adani Solar** | 4+ GW | Vertically integrated; largest |
| **Waaree Energies** | ~5 GW | Market leader; deep OEM relationships |
| **Premier Energies** | ~2 GW | Diversified customer base |
| **Vikram Solar** | ~1.5 GW | Strong module brand |
| **Websol Energy** | 1.2 GW | Pure-play cell manufacturer; highest ROCE |

**Websol's Niche:** Unlike peers who are module-focussed, Websol is primarily a **solar cell manufacturer** — modules are a secondary/vertical integration play. This gives it pricing power in the cell market where DCR cell supply is constrained.

### Key Growth Drivers

- **ALMM List II enforcement** — expanding the eligible domestic manufacturing list
- **ALMM List III (June 2028)** — will require domestic ingot/wafer sourcing; Websol is preparing with Linton MOU
- **India's 500 GW renewable target by 2030** — implies ~30 GW/year solar installation
- **Production Linked Incentive (PLI)** — scheme for domestic solar manufacturing

## 15. Bull vs Bear Case

### 🟢 Bull Case (6 Arguments)

1. **India's solar manufacturing story is structural, not cyclical** — 500GW target by 2030, ALMM policy, PLI schemes. Websol is a direct beneficiary with proven execution.
2. **Extraordinary return ratios** — 63% ROCE and 67% ROE are among the highest in Indian manufacturing. Capital deployed is earning exceptional returns.
3. **Deep value at 14.4x P/E** — Trading at half the industry P/E (30.1x) and below Waaree (19.8x) despite superior ROCE. The discount is unwarranted for the long term.
4. **Turnaround is real and sustainable** — From near-zero revenue to ₹1,049 Cr in 2 years. Order book of ₹1,161 Cr provides FY27 visibility.
5. **Balance sheet transformed** — Net cash position, zero debt dependency for operations. Phase 3 capex can be funded through CFO + debt at manageable D/E.
6. **TOPCon + Phase 3 = Step-change** — By FY28, capacity of 5+ GW positions Websol as a top-4 Indian solar manufacturer. Current MCap of ₹4,375 Cr does not price this.

### 🔴 Bear Case (6 Arguments)

1. **Promoter pledge is existential risk** — 89.4% of promoter shares pledged. If IREDA forces a sale, stock could collapse 40-50%. This is the single reason institutions stay away.
