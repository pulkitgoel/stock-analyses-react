## 1. Business Overview

Palantir Technologies builds and deploys **operating systems for organizations** — software that enables data-driven decision-making at scale. Founded in 2003 by Peter Thiel, Alex Karp (CEO), Stephen Cohen, and others.

**Three Core Platforms:**

1. **Gotham** — The original product. Built for defense, intelligence, and government agencies. Used for military operations, counter-terrorism, threat analysis. Since renamed and folded into the broader AIP platform.

2. **Foundry** — The enterprise data operating system. Used by commercial enterprises to integrate siloed data, build ontologies, and drive operational decisions. Customers: Airbus, BP, Merck, Rio Tinto, etc.

3. **AIP (Artificial Intelligence Platform)** — Launched commercially in 2023. The key growth driver. Enables enterprises and government agencies to deploy LLMs over their own proprietary data without exposing sensitive information. Integrates with GPT, Gemini, Claude, and other models.

**Key Differentiator:** Palantir isn't a model company — it's an **integration and operations platform** that lets organizations actually *use* AI in their workflows. CEO Alex Karp: *"We are different from model companies who engage in an intensely competitive race... Our path has been different, building a juggernaut that delivers results."*

**Customer Count:** 1,007 commercial customers (TTM ending Mar 2026), up 31% YoY.

## 4. Competitive Moat & Strategy

### Why Palantir Can't Be Easily Replicated

1. **Data Integration Complexity** — Palantir spent 20 years building the infrastructure to integrate messy, siloed data. This isn't something a startup can replicate quickly.

2. **Government Contracts as Moat** — The U.S. Army TITAN contract ($10B over 10 years) creates a deep, sticky revenue stream that competitors can't easily access due to security clearances and integration complexity.

3. **The "AI Boot Camp" Funnel** — Palantir runs intensive multi-day workshops where corporate teams build live prototypes on its platform. This converts prospects into customers with real proof-of-value before they sign.

4. **Platform Stickiness** — Once an organization builds its ontology, workflows, and operational processes on Foundry/AIP, switching costs are enormous.

5. **Model Agnostic** — Palantir doesn't compete with OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google. It *integrates* all of them. As Karp said: *"Token costs have suffered a thousandfold decline... winners and losers swap places every six months."* Palantir sits above the fray.

| **Maven Smart System (MSS)** | Operational (2026) | Replaced 9 separate DoD systems. $1.3B contract ceiling through 2029. **20,000+ active users** across 35 military services. Doubled users since Jan 2026. |

### The Maven Story — Transformational for National Security

What happened in Feb-Mar 2026 with **Operation Epic Fury** is a watershed moment for Palantir:

In three weeks, the US military struck **5,500-6,000 targets in Iran**. The first 1,000 strikes happened in **24 hours**. The system enabling this? Palantir's Maven Smart System — a platform that fused **9 separate military intelligence systems** into one interface and compressed the kill chain from **hours to minutes**.

The Pentagon's Chief Digital and AI Officer, Cameron Stanley, demonstrated the system publicly: *"Left click, right click, left click, magically it becomes a detection. This is revolutionary."*

**Context:** Project Maven started in 2017 with a $9M Google contract. Google employees revolted (4,000 signed a letter, ~12 resigned), Google didn't renew, and **Palantir stepped in**. By May 2024, Palantir won the MSS contract ($480M over 5 years). By 2025, the ceiling was raised to $1.3B. By 2026, it's operational at scale with 20,000+ users.

The Pentagon has now designated Maven a **"Program of Record"** — meaning it's no longer experimental but formalized within the military's budgeting and acquisition system with guaranteed continued funding.

**Why this matters for investors:** This isn't just a contract win — it's Palantir becoming **core infrastructure for US military operations**. The switching cost for the DoD to replace Maven is now effectively infinite. Every conflict that uses MSS successfully is a reference sale for allied nations (NATO, Five Eyes, etc.).
