### 🌍 Global Spillover Stories Affecting India

#### 🔴 IOC & MRPL Halt Iraq Oil Loadings
**IOC** and **MRPL** have suspended crude oil loadings from Iraq due to mounting security risks in the Strait of Hormuz. IOC abandoned plans to load a supertanker from Basrah Oil Terminal. This is a direct, real-world impact of the Hormuz crisis on Indian energy security.

| Stock | Impact | Reason |
|-------|--------|--------|
| IOC | 🔴 Negative | Forced to source costlier spot crude; disruption to Iraq supply (traditionally 10-15% of India's crude imports) |
| MRPL | 🔴 Negative | Dependent on Persian Gulf crude; supply disruption hurts refining margins |
| BPCL | 🔴 Negative | Also sources Iraq crude; alternative sourcing costs higher |
| HPCL | 🔴 Negative | Rising crude hurts under-recoveries; government may delay price pass-through |
| ONGC | 🟢 Positive | Higher crude oil realization for domestically produced oil; substitution demand benefits |
| OIL | 🟢 Positive | Higher crude price environment benefits upstream E&P companies |

#### 🔴 Trump Generic Drug Tariff — Indian Pharma Stocks Crushed
Trump announced a **phased generic drug tariff** via Truth Social on July 22:
- Zero tariffs until Aug 1, 2028 (2-year grace)
- 100% tariff from Aug 1, 2028
- 200% tariff from Aug 1, 2029

Indian pharma exports to the US are ~$10B, and India supplies **40% of US generic drugs** by volume. While the 2-year window provides breathing room, the long-term trajectory is clearly negative.

| Stock | Impact | Reason |
|-------|--------|--------|
| SUNPHARMA | 🔴 Negative | 30%+ US revenue exposure; large generic portfolio; fell 1.91% |
| LUPIN | 🔴 Negative | ~40% US revenue; generic-heavy; led pharma decliners |
| CIPLA | 🔴 Negative | Major US generics presence; -1.56% on the day |
| DRREDDY | 🔴 Negative | Strong US generics footprint; fell 2.16% |
| AUROPHARMA | 🔴 Negative | 50%+ US revenue; largest US generic manufacturing footprint among Indian cos |
| ZYDUSLIFE | 🔴 Negative | Growing US generics book; tariff headwind |
| ALEMBIC | 🔴 Negative | US generics dependent; Nifty 500 decliner |
| GLENMARK | 🔴 Negative | US generics exposure; fell 1-3% |
| GLAND | 🔴 Negative | Injectable generics; could be tariff target despite complexity |
| BIOCON | 🔴 Negative | Biosimilars exposed to U.S. market; Malaysia-based manufacturing doesn't help |
| MARKSANS | 🔴 Negative | Down 7-13% (sharpest); small-cap generic exposed |

**Note:** The **2-year grace period** means NO immediate earnings impact. However, long-term structural threat to the Indian generic pharma business model. Companies with **local U.S. manufacturing** (Aurobindo, Dr. Reddy's, Lupin, Cipla, Zydus) may be better positioned to adapt. **Alkem** and **Torrent** (fully India-dependent) face the highest risk.
