## 2. SEBI Reintroduces Open Market Buyback — Effective Aug 1, 2026

**What happened:** SEBI approved the **reintroduction of open market share buybacks through the stock exchange** route, effective **August 1, 2026**. Key features:
- Companies can buy back shares through open market (stock exchange) OR tender offer OR book-building
- Buyback must be completed within **66 working days** from opening
- **At least 40% of funds** must be utilised within the first half of the buyback period
- **Promoters not allowed** to participate in open market buyback
- Replaces the previous tax regime where buybacks were taxed as deemed dividend

**Why this matters for markets:**
- Open market buyback was the **second most popular buyback method** before it was eliminated
- More companies expected to announce buybacks, providing **demand support** for stocks
- Positive for **high FCF, low-debt companies** with excess cash
- Particularly beneficial for **PSU stocks** where government can buy back shares without reducing stake below 51%

## 3. India Exports Likely to Cross $500 Billion in FY27

**What happened:** According to government projections (via NaMo App), India's goods exports are expected to remain strong in FY27, likely crossing **$500 billion** — supported by new trade agreements, diversified export sectors, and stronger global demand. The Commerce Ministry is targeting $1 trillion in total exports (goods + services).

**Key details:**
- FY26 goods exports closed at ~$440 billion
- Target: $500B+ for FY27 (need ~$60B incremental growth)
- India-UK CETA, India-Australia ECTA, and new FTA pushes driving growth
- Textiles, leather, gems & jewellery, auto components leading the charge
