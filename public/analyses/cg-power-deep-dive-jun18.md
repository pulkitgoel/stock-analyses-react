## 1. Business Overview

CG Power & Industrial Solutions is a **Murugappa Group** company (124-year-old conglomerate) promoted by **Tube Investments of India Ltd**. It's a global enterprise providing end-to-end solutions to utilities, industries, and consumers for the management and application of efficient and sustainable electrical energy.

**Two Main Business Segments:**

1. **Power Systems** — Transformers (up to 765kV, expanding to 1200kV), Switchgears (including GIS up to 400kV), Instrument Transformers, Circuit Breakers, Lightning Arrestors. Focus: Power T&D, utilities, data centres, export markets.

2. **Industrial Systems** — Motors (IE2/IE3/IE4), Drives & Automation (AMX series), Railways (GG Tronics - Kavach systems), Industrial Controls. Focus: Industrial, SME, Railways, export markets.

**New Growth Engines:**
- **CG Semi (OSAT)** — Sanand, Gujarat. G1 facility operational (0.5M chips/day). G2 under construction (14.5M chips/day by end-CY2026)
- **Fabless Semiconductor design** — Acquired from Renesas Electronics for ₹284 Cr
- **Switchgear Greenfield** — ₹748 Cr capex approved for MV/EHV switchgears
- **Transformer Capacity Expansion** — From 17,000 MVA to 1,10,000+ MVA by end-CY2026

## 14. Final Verdict

CG Power is executing exceptionally well in its **Power Systems** segment — the order book, margin expansion, and capacity additions tell a compelling story. The **turnaround in Motors** is encouraging. The **OSAT bet** adds optionality with government backing.

**However, the valuation is the elephant in the room.** At 122x P/E with a 10.3% 3Y profit CAGR, the PEG of 11.9 means you're paying a very heavy price for growth. The stock needs to sustain 20%+ earnings growth for the valuation to normalize over time.

**What needs to happen for the stock to work from here:**
1. Power Systems growth sustains at 20%+ (highly likely given ₹12,644 Cr backlog)
2. OSAT losses start narrowing (G2 completion + govt grant recognition)
3. Industrial Systems margins recover to 12%+ (motors turnaround materializes)
4. No major global recession that hits order inflows

**The Murugappa Group factor** is not to be underestimated — they took CG from near-bankruptcy (FY20: -₹1,331 Cr PAT) to record profitability in 6 years. The governance premium is real.

**Rating:** ⚠️ **Expensive, but quality business.** Not a value buy at current levels. Wait for a dip to ₹800-850 (near 50 DMA / pivot) for a better entry. If you're already invested, hold — the Power Systems momentum and OSAT catalyst could drive further upside. The ₹900 Cr US data centre order proves CG is winning in premium global markets.

*This is a "best-in-class" Indian electrical equipment company with a strong parent, but the market has priced in a lot of perfection already.*
