## 🏛️ FM Sitharaman Pushes Toy Industry to Capture 25% of Global Market

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman on July 7 addressed the 17th Toy Biz International B2B Expo in Delhi, urging the Indian toy sector to target **25% of the projected $179 billion global toy market by 2032** — far above the current $5 billion target. She credited policy measures (60% import duty hike since 2020, BIS enforcement, National Action Plan for Toys) for India's toy exports reaching **$186 million in FY26** and imports declining **71% from 2019 levels**.

The government is promoting mega textile/toy parks, cluster development across Karnataka, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, UP, and Andhra Pradesh under the MSME SPURTI scheme.

## 🏛️ SEBI Amends FPI Regulations — Fee Payments Now in INR

SEBI has notified amendments to FPI Regulations, replacing the US dollar-based fee system with a rupee-linked structure (effective in 6 months). Key changes:
- Registration fee: $1,000 → ₹90,000 equivalent
- Category-I FPI fee: $2,500 → ₹2.3 lakh
- Late/continuation fees also revised to INR
- Custodians now pay ₹85K/month instead of ₹10L annually
- Mutual funds allowed intraday borrowing for settlement gaps

The move simplifies fee collection and addresses operational issues with USD-denominated accounting. SEBI collected $12.98M in FY26 through FPI fees.

## 🏛️ SEBI Issues Administrative Warning to Reliance Industries Over Insider Trading

## 🏛️ SEBI Reintroduces Open Market Buyback Framework (Effective Aug 1, 2026)

SEBI notified the Buy-Back of Securities (Amendment) Regulations, 2026 (Jul 6), re-introducing open market buybacks through stock exchanges effective **August 1, 2026**. Key provisions:
- Buyback cap: 15% of paid-up capital + free reserves (standalone & consolidated)
- VWAP-based pricing with escrow arrangements
- 4-day start window, 66-day completion cap
- Promoter holding freeze at ISIN level
- No mandatory merchant banker requirement

This follows taxation changes in the Finance Act, 2026 that make buybacks more attractive.
