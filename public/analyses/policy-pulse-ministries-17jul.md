## 🏛️ SEBI Buy-Back of Securities (Amendment) Regulations, 2026

**Notified:** July 1, 2026 | **Effective:** August 1, 2026

SEBI has notified sweeping amendments to the buy-back framework. The most consequential changes:

1. **Open-market buyback route revived** (was abolished April 1, 2025) — effective August 1, 2026. Buyback must open within 4 working days of announcement and close within 66 working days.
2. **Merchant Banker appointment made optional** — companies can now self-execute buybacks
3. **Direct shareholder intimation** mandated within 1 working day via electronic mode
4. **Promoter group ISIN-level freeze** during buyback period (governance measure)

**Implication:** Cash-rich companies now have an easier, cheaper route to return capital to shareholders. The revived open-market route is particularly favorable for mid-sized companies where tender offers are procedurally burdensome.

## RBI Capital Market Exposure Norms — Effective July 1, 2026

The RBI's revised Capital Market Exposure (CME) framework took effect July 1, 2026 after a three-month deferral from April. Key changes:

- **100% collateral on bank guarantees** provided to brokers for margin funding
- **Tighter broker funding norms** — banks cannot fund unregulated activities
- **Stringent Loan Against Shares (LAS) LTV limits** — reduced leverage availability
- **Net-worth based underwriting exposure limits** for capital market intermediaries

**Ongoing impact assessment (now in effect for 17 days):** This is a structural headwind for trading volumes. Retail margin funding is more expensive, which reduces trading frequency and size. Exchange revenues (transaction-based) face volume compression.
