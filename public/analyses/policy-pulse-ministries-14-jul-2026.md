**Two regulatory developments with direct stock market implications: (1) SEBI tightens conflict-of-interest rules for employees with stricter investment disclosure norms, and (2) DPIIT issues revised FDI approval SOP with enhanced national security scrutiny on land-border country investments.**

## 1. SEBI Notifies Stricter Conflict-of-Interest Rules

SEBI introduced a comprehensive conflict-of-interest framework for employees: tighter investment restrictions, enhanced disclosure (family members, relatives, previous 3 years professional interests, properties, investments), two-year cooling-off period, and gifts over ₹50,000 must be reported. Triggered by Hindenburg Research allegations against former Chairperson Madhabi Puri Buch.

## 2. DPIIT Issues Revised FDI Approval SOP

DPIIT issued a revised Standard Operating Procedure (superseding 2023 SOP) for processing FDI proposals requiring government approval. Enhanced disclosure obligations for Land Border Country (LBC) linked investments. Below 10% LBC ownership: reporting through FIF/NSWS portal, no prior approval. Follows Press Note 2 of 2026.
