## Business Overview

Azad Engineering is a global Tier-1 supplier of highly-engineered, complex, mission & life-critical precision forged and machined components for **Aerospace & Defence**, **Energy** (gas/steam/nuclear turbines), and **Oil & Gas** industries. The company is headquartered in Hyderabad and was incorporated in 1983.

**Customers:** Supplies to 6 major aerospace OEMs (GE, Honeywell, Rolls-Royce, Pratt & Whitney, Eaton, Safran) and 5 of the world's largest turbine manufacturers (GE Vernova, Siemens Energy, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, Baker Hughes, MAN Energy Solutions).

**Key Stats:**
- ~93% export revenue
- Portfolio of 1,700+ qualified parts
- 45+ manufacturing processes
- Tier-1 supplier status for critical engine components

- **189 debtor days** and **73% inventory jump** suggest product isn't moving fast enough despite order book claims

- "25%+" guidance implies **deceleration** from recent 54% PAT growth trajectory
- **Low ROE (9.09%)** suggests new capex isn't yet generating adequate returns
- **No dividend ever** = shareholder returns entirely dependent on price appreciation

## Final Verdict: FAVORABLE WITH CAUTION 🟡

Azad Engineering is executing an extremely capital-intensive capacity expansion story. The ₹6,500 Cr order book provides rare multi-year visibility, and the OEM contract wins (especially MHI single-source) demonstrate world-class technical capabilities. Both FIIs and DIIs are aggressively accumulating.

However, the financial reality is harsh: **-₹1,934 Mn FCF, 189 debtor days, and promoter dilution of 10% in 2 years** are genuine concerns. At **98x P/E**, the market is pricing in execution perfection.

The investment case rests entirely on management's ability to **normalize working capital in FY27**. If CFO turns positive by H2 FY27 as guided, the **PEG of 0.65** makes this look cheap. But if cash burn continues, the multiple compression could be severe.

- ✅ Understanding that near-term returns depend entirely on execution
