## 🌍 USTR Section 301 Forced Labor Tariffs: 12.5% Proposed on India — Negative for Pharma, Textiles, IT

**Date:** 03 Jul 2026

## Executive Summary

The Office of the United States Trade Representative (USTR) has proposed **12.5% additional tariffs** on imports from **54 economies** — including India, China, Vietnam, Japan, South Korea, Brazil, Switzerland, and the UK — under **Section 301** investigations into forced labor import enforcement. The comment deadline is **July 6, 2026**, with hearings on **July 7, 2026**. A final decision is expected in **late July**, potentially before the Section 122 tariffs expire on July 24.

This is a **direct negative** for Indian exports to the US market. Export-oriented Indian sectors — pharma, textiles, and IT services — face margin compression and competitive disadvantage vs. countries with lower proposed rates or exemptions.

## 🛡️ Bilateral Trade Agreement (BTA) — The Escape Valve

India is currently negotiating a Bilateral Trade Agreement with the US, with US Deputy USTR Brendan Lynch in New Delhi for talks (ending June 4). The agreement framework (announced Feb 6, 2026) already reduced US tariffs on Indian goods from 50% to 18%.

**India's ask:**
1. Terminate Section 301 investigations through the BTA framework
2. Secure tariff rates lower than competitors
3. Extend duty-free access for pharma generics

**Likelihood of relief:** Medium-high. India has leverage through:
- Defence cooperation pivot (India stopped buying Russian oil per Feb 2026 deal)
- Geopolitical alignment against China
- $500B purchasing intent commitment
- Strong bilateral relationship with Trump
