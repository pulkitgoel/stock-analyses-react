## 🔴 Primary Trigger: Accenture Guidance Cut

**The event:** Accenture reported Q3 FY26 on June 18 and **cut its full-year revenue growth guidance** from 3-5% to 3-4% in constant currency (or 4-5% excluding US federal business impact).

**Key numbers:**
- Q3 revenue: $18.4B (in line)
- Q4 guidance: $17.75-18.4B (below Street expectation of $18.47B)
- New bookings: $19.32B (−2% YoY)
- **Outsourcing bookings fell 15% YoY** — the single scariest number for Indian IT
- Headcount grew (positive), but consulting demand remains weak
- Client budgets are NOT expanding — spend is being *redeployed* to AI, not *increased*

**Accenture stock crashed 17-19%** on NYSE. Indian IT ADRs followed:
- Infosys ADR: −10%
- Wipro ADR: −4%

**Why this matters for Indian IT:** Accenture is the global bellwether for IT services. Its guidance cut implies the demand environment remains soft, and Indian IT companies (which are *more* exposed to low-end outsourcing) will face similar or worse pressure.

## 🟡 Secondary Factor 1: Hawkish Fed (Jun 18)

The US Federal Reserve struck a **hawkish tone** at its June 18 meeting, raising concerns about US tech spending. Higher-for-longer rates mean:
- 🟡 Enterprises delay large IT transformation projects
- 🟡 IT budgets remain constrained → fewer large deal wins
- 🟡 USD strength hurts repatriated earnings for Indian IT

---

## 🟡 Secondary Factor 2: Anthropic AI Disruption Fears

Anthropic (makers of Claude) launched an **AI legal tool** that automates contract review, NDA triage, compliance workflows, and legal briefing. This reignited fears that **agentic AI is directly replacing the work Indian IT firms bill for**.

**The structural concern:** 30-40% of Indian IT revenue comes from application development, maintenance & testing — the exact segments most vulnerable to AI automation. Estimates suggest 30-50% productivity gains could structurally erase **9-12% of sector revenue over 3-4 years**.

This is NOT a cyclical slowdown — this is a **structural repricing of India's $250B outsourcing model**.

The market had rallied **4-5% over 5 sessions** after the US-Iran peace deal. Investors used the Accenture trigger as an excuse to book profits. Given the Nifty IT was already down 25%+ YTD, the selling snowballed because:
