## 🌍 US-Canada Trade War: 50% Section 338 Tariffs (Jul 20)

Trump signed **three proclamations** imposing **50% tariffs** on Canadian goods — first use of Section 338 (Tariff Act 1930) in nearly a century.

**Coverage:** Motor vehicles, alcoholic beverages, dairy + wine, hockey sticks, cement, swimming pools, furniture, fishing rods, seeds, clothing, wigs, etc.
**Magnitude:** ~$20B of imports (5.2% of total US imports from Canada)
**Effective:** 30 days from signing (mid-August 2026)
**Excluded:** Energy, potash, critical minerals, goods already under Section 232

## 📅 Section 122 Tariff — 3 Days to Expiry (Jul 24, 2026)

The **10-15% global import surcharge** expires by statute on **July 24, 2026**. The 150-day limit under Trade Act of 1974 is non-extendable by the President alone.

### What Changes
- **Duty-free gap possible** between Jul 24 expiry and replacement tariff activation
- Proposed replacement: **12.5% Section 301 tariffs on 46 countries** (already in pipeline)
- EU already moved to 15% deal ceiling (Jul 1)
