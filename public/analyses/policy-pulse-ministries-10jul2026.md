## 🏛️ RBI Capital Market Exposure Norms Take Effect — Trading Volumes Already Dipping

RBI's **revised capital market exposure norms kicked in on July 1, 2026**, and the impact is already visible across Indian markets:

**What changed:**
- Bank guarantees to brokers now require **100% collateral** (up from 50%)
- Proprietary trading funding requires **100% cash collateral**
- **Loan-against-shares capped at 60% LTV** (Loan-to-Value)
- IPO loans capped at ₹25 lakh per borrower

**Early market impact:**
- Brokers who relied on cheap bank leverage for prop trading and margin funding are now **capital-starved**
- Early data shows **trading volumes are already dipping**
- If volumes continue falling, **bid-ask spreads widen**, execution worsens, and market liquidity thins — especially in **mid and smallcaps** where prop desks provided crucial depth
- This disproportionately impacts NSE/BSE transaction revenue and broker profitability

**Background:** RBI doubled collateral requirements as part of broader financial stability measures. The **Financial Stability Report (June 2026)** named AI-enabled cyber threats as the top risk for banks, and warned AI-driven asset price correction could pose systemic risk through indirect exposure.

## 🏛️ SEBI Revives Open-Market Buyback Route — Effective August 1, 2026

SEBI notified the **SEBI (Buy-Back of Securities) (Amendment) Regulations, 2026** on July 1, effective **August 1, 2026**. Key changes:

- **Open market route revived** at a lower ceiling: Buy-backs must be **less than 15%** of paid-up capital and free reserves (tested standalone AND consolidated)
- **New MPS safeguard:** No buy-back may breach minimum public shareholding norms
- **Execution timelines:** Offers must open within **4 working days** of public announcement and close within **66 working days**

**Why it matters:** The open-market buyback route was previously restricted. Its revival gives companies a powerful tool to return cash to shareholders, support stock prices, and improve shareholder returns — particularly for cash-rich companies.

## 🏛️ SEBI Revises ETF Trading Framework — Base Price, Price Bands, Pre-Open Call Auction

SEBI revised the regulatory framework for ETFs on **June 15, 2026** (Circular HO/47/11/11(1)2026-MRD-POD3). Key changes:

- Revised method for determining ETF **base price** — moving away from T-2 NAV reliance
- Updated **price bands** for ETF trading
- Improved **pre-open call auction mechanism** and close-out procedures
- Aims to address pricing inefficiencies from existing T-2 NAV-based pricing

## 🏛️ SEBI Notifies Custodian Fee Shift from Annual to Monthly — Effective Oct 1

SEBI Notification No. SEBI/LAD-NRO/GN/2026/308 (July 3, 2026) replaces the annual custodian fee structure:
- **Old:** ₹10,00,000 or 0.0005% of AUC annually
- **New:** ₹85,000 or 0.0000416% of AUC **monthly** (payable within 15 days of month-end)
- Effective **October 1, 2026**

**Impact:** Marginally increases compliance overhead for custodians but does not materially change total fee burden.
