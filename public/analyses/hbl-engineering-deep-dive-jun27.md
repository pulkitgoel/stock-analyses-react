## Business Overview

HBL Engineering (formerly HBL Power Systems) designs, manufactures, and services a diversified range of products across three key segments:

**1. Industrial Batteries (~71% of FY25 revenue)**
- Lead-Acid (VRLA & PLT): Telecom, railways, UPS, data servers, defence
- Nickel-Cadmium: #2 globally in Ni-Cd batteries; strong export growth (+40% in FY25) driven by Middle East OEM/EPC clients
- Lithium: Becoming price-competitive with VRLA; supplies Vande Bharat trains, Siemens Germany, global defence forces, Indian defence for undersea vessel projects. Grew 37% FY23→FY25

**2. Railway Signalling & Kavach (Electronics — fastest growing)**
- Kavach (Train Collision Avoidance System) — Indian Railways' indigenous safety system
- HBL is one of only 2 qualified vendors for Kavach v4.0
- This segment has delivered a step-change in scale: ₹4,000+ Cr order book in FY26
- Q3 FY26 revenue surge to ₹1,223 Cr (44% OPM) was driven by Kavach execution

**3. Defence & Aviation**
- Advanced battery systems for submarines, aircraft, tactical communication
- Growing with Make-in-India and defence indigenisation programmes

**Key Stats:**
- Incorporated: 1983 (formerly Hyderabad Batteries Ltd, merged with SAB Nife Power Systems)
- Global #2 in Ni-Cd batteries
- 45+ manufacturing processes
- Zero promoter pledge, near debt-free balance sheet

## Final Verdict: FAVORABLE WITH CAUTION 🟡

HBL Engineering is a high-quality business with extraordinary return ratios (ROCE 58.4%, ROE 45.3%, D/E 0.03) and a record FCF of ₹618 Cr. The Kavach order book of ₹4,000+ Cr provides strong near-term visibility, and the PEG of 0.26 is objectively cheap for a company growing PAT at 105% 3Y CAGR.

However, the stock is down 26% from its 52W high despite record earnings — the market is pricing in the risk that FY26 was a peak earnings year. The Q4 margin compression to 12% OPM (from 44% in Sep'25) and management's cautious FY27 guidance reinforce this concern. Quarterly volatility of this magnitude makes any static PE-based valuation unreliable.

The bull case rests on Kavach execution sustaining at scale, battery margins stabilizing, and FIIs continuing to return. The bear case rests on FY26 being a peak driven by lumpy Kavach deliveries that cannot be repeated at the same margin profile.
