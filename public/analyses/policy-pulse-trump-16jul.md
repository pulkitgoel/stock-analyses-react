## 🇺🇸 Section 122 Tariff Expiry Countdown: 8 Days to Go

The **10% global import surcharge** imposed under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 is **set to expire by operation of law on July 24, 2026** — just 8 days from today. This 150-day tariff, initially set at 10% (later raised to 15%), was Trump's response after the Supreme Court struck down his IEEPA tariff authority in February.

### What Happens After July 24?

Three scenarios are being debated:
1. **Automatic Expiry** — Rates drop to zero for most countries (Section 301 tariffs on China remain, as do Section 232 steel/aluminum tariffs)
2. **Congressional Extension** — Unlikely given gridlock
3. **Replacement via Section 301 or new authority** — USTR already proposed 12.5% tariffs on 60 countries including India over forced labor findings

### 🌍 Middle East / Strait of Hormuz Escalation

Renewed US-Iran hostilities around the Strait of Hormuz continue to roil oil markets. Brent crude recently jumped 4%+ after Iranian missiles struck a Qatari LNG carrier and two other tankers. The chokepoint handles ~20% of global oil supply. Oil prices remain elevated despite being down ~33% from April peaks. The US has restarted its blockade after Iran attacks.

### 🗣️ Trump Primetime Speech Tonight

President Trump is scheduled to deliver a prime-time address on election security and voting machines — political in nature but could create volatility in election-adjacent sectors.
