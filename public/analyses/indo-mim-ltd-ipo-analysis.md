## 1. Business Overview

**Indo-MIM Limited** (founded 1996, headquartered in Bengaluru) is the **world's largest manufacturer of precision engineering components using Metal Injection Molding (MIM) technology**, holding a **6.8% global market share** by MIM revenue (F&S Report) — a position it has held for **six consecutive years**.

### What is MIM?

Metal Injection Molding is a specialised manufacturing process that combines the design freedom of plastic injection molding with the strength and durability of metal. Fine metal powder is mixed with a binder, injected into a precision mold, and then processed to produce small, structurally strong components — ideal for parts too complex or too small to machine economically at scale.

### Advanced Technologies

Beyond MIM, Indo-MIM has built capabilities across:
- **Investment Casting** — high-tolerance metal parts
- **Precision Machining** — CNC finishing and assembly
- **Ceramic Injection Molding (CIM)** — for wear-resistant components
- **3D Metal Printing** — additive manufacturing for prototyping and low-volume production

### End-User Segments (FY26 Revenue Split)

| **Automotive** | ~24.6% | Safety systems, fuel/powertrain, interior components |
| **Defence** | ~18.7% | Firearm components: triggers, hammers, sights |
| **Medical** | ~18.1% | Endoscopy, laparoscopy, dental robotics, orthopaedics |
| **Aerospace** | ~12.0% | Manifolds, housings, nozzles, locking rings, brackets |
| **Consumer** | ~10.8% | Fashion accessories, cellphone parts, tools, hardware |
| **Others** | ~15.9% | Powder sales, traded products, tooling |

### Global Footprint

- **15 manufacturing facilities** across 4 countries: India (6), USA (6), UK (2), Mexico (1)
- **Sales offices** in China, Germany, USA
- **Sales representatives** across Europe (Czech Republic, France, Italy, Poland, Turkey) and Asia (Japan, South Korea, Israel)
- Serves **1,100+ customers** across **55 countries**
- **77% export revenue** (North America ~44%, Europe significant)

### Key Competitive Strengths

1. **World's largest installed MIM capacity** — six years running
2. **Sticky customer relationships** — 91.6% of FY26 revenue from repeat customers
3. **High switching costs** — 2-3 year qualification cycle for OEMs; dedicated tooling per part
4. **Dual-shore manufacturing** — low-cost India base + local-for-local in US/UK/Mexico
5. **80+ alloy options** — extensive materials expertise
6. **6,400+ products** manufactured — deep production maturity
7. **Backward integrated** — mold design, tooling, finishing, assembly in-house
