## 1. USTR Forced Labor Tariff Hearings — Jul 7-9, 2026

**What happened:** The U.S. Trade Representative opens public hearings July 7-9 on proposed Section 301 tariffs against **60 economies** found to have failed in enforcing forced labor import prohibitions. USTR has proposed **12.5% tariff** on over 50 countries including India, with a lower 10% tier for others. The hearings begin tomorrow, with the Section 122 global 10% tariff expiring **July 24, 2026**.

**Key details:**
- USTR hearings: July 7-9 (Tue-Thu)
- Current 10% Section 122 tariff expires: July 24
- Replacement: Proposed 12.5% forced labor tariffs (tier 2) on India, China, Vietnam, Thailand, Japan, South Korea + others
- Second probe (structural overcapacity): Still pending — being used as bargaining chip for bilateral deals
- India-US trade deal negotiations ongoing but stalled pending final U.S. tariff plan
- India seeking "comparative advantage" over China, Vietnam, Bangladesh, ASEAN

**Irony:** The 10% → 12.5% increase may actually **benefit Indian exporters** vs competitors if it's uniformly applied — India's competitors (Bangladesh, Vietnam, China) would face the same increase. Indian exporters "heave a sigh of relief" per TOI.

## 2. US Pharma Tariffs — 100% on Patented Drugs, Generics Excluded

**What happened:** Trump's April 2, 2026 Section 232 Proclamation imposes **100% ad valorem tariff** on imported patented pharmaceutical products and APIs. However, **generic pharmaceuticals, biosimilars, and their associated ingredients are expressly excluded**. The tariff took effect July 31, 2026 for 17 named large pharma companies, and September 29, 2026 for all others.

**What this means for India:**
- India's core pharma export business is **overwhelmingly generic** — NOT directly affected
- A White House official specifically confirmed generics "shall not be subject to Section 232 tariffs at this time"
- Indian API exporters (Divi's, Aurobindo, Dr Reddy's) to US-bound formulators remain on competitive footing

## 3. USMCA Not Renewed — Annual Reviews Begin

**What happened:** The Trump administration declined to renew the USMCA trade agreement (Trump's own 2020 replacement for NAFTA). The agreement doesn't expire immediately, but goes into annual reviews with a 10-year clock toward expiration. Effectively, the US is the lone holdout as the agreement continues on autopilot.

**Stock impact:** Minimal direct India impact. However, it signals continued US unilateralism on trade.

## 4. Brazil Tariff Deadline — July 15

**What happened:** The US has until July 15 to decide whether to impose proposed 25% tariffs on Brazilian goods. Senator Flavio Bolsonaro is lobbying Washington to delay until after Brazil's October election. Exempt products: beef, coffee, rare earths, aircraft parts.

**Stock impact:** Low direct India impact, but sets precedent for US tariff action against trading partners.
