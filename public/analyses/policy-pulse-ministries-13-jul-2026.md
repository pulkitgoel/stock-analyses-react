## 🏛️ USTR Proposes 12.5% Additional Tariff on 44 Countries Including India — Forced Labor Allegations

The **Office of the US Trade Representative (USTR)** has proposed **Section 301 tariffs of 12.5%** on imports from 44 countries, including India, alleging these nations have failed to effectively prohibit goods produced with forced labor from entering global supply chains. An additional 16 countries face a **10% tariff** under the same framework.

**India's response:**
- India has **formally challenged** the proposed tariff, arguing:
  - The USTR failed to provide sufficient evidence linking India to the alleged concerns
  - The investigation lacks a detailed, country-specific assessment
  - Applying blanket tariffs constitutes unjustified generalization
  - India's legal framework already prohibits forced labor
- India wants these concerns addressed through the **ongoing India-US Bilateral Trade Agreement (BTA)** negotiations
- **22 Democratic state attorneys general** have filed objections to the proposed tariffs
- US textile groups are urging a **"regional sourcing credit"** mechanism to offset the tariffs through US textile purchases

**What's at stake:** If implemented, these tariffs would be layered on top of the existing 10% baseline tariff and any additional tariffs from the excess industrial capacity probes.

## 🏛️ June Retail Inflation Rises to 4.38% — Breaches RBI's 4% Target After 5 Months

India's **June retail inflation** rose to **4.38%**, up from 3.93% in May, breaching the RBI's 4% target for the first time in five months. The data released Monday shows inflationary pressures re-emerging.

**Key context:**
- RBI had projected inflation at ~4.6% for FY26-27 (as per April MPC)
- The Iran-US war is expected to cast influence on inflation forecasts through elevated crude prices
- RBI kept repo rate unchanged at 5.25% in April MPC with a neutral stance
- 125 bps cumulative cuts since Feb 2025 were expected to be the easing cycle peak
- Inflation breach reduces room for further rate cuts in the near term

## 🏛️ SEBI Tightens Employee Conduct Rules — Stricter Investment Norms and Cooling-Off Period

SEBI has introduced a **revamped code of conduct** for its employees, effective immediately:

- Employees must **exit or freeze non-permitted investments**
- **Wider disclosure requirements** for employee investments
- **Cooling-off period** introduced for former employees joining regulated entities
- Aims to prevent conflicts of interest and insider trading risks

**Market impact:** This is an internal SEBI governance reform — minimal direct stock market impact. It signals SEBI's intent to maintain regulatory integrity rather than any policy shift for market participants.

No stock-level inferences — regulatory governance change only.
