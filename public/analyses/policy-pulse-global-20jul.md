## 1. 🔥 US-Iran Full-Scale War Declaration

**What happened:** Iran's President Masoud Pezeshkian declared the country is in a "full-scale war" after the US launched its ninth consecutive night of airstrikes. The tit-for-tat escalation follows the collapse of the interim ceasefire (June 14 MoU) in early July.

**Key facts:**
- US struck military command centers, air defense, coastal surveillance, missile/drone sites around Tabriz and Bushehr
- Iran retaliated by striking Bahrain (US 5th Fleet HQ) and Kuwait
- **Iran-allied Houthis in Yemen declared a maritime blockade of Saudi ports** — closing the Red Sea/Bab el-Mandeb alternative route used to bypass Hormuz
- A third US service member was killed in Iraq
- 17 US service members have died since the war began in February
- Oil tanker caught fire in the southern corridor the US encouraged ships to use

**⚠️ The Houthi blockade threat is a material escalation:** Saudi Arabia had diverted millions of barrels/day via Red Sea pipeline to bypass Hormuz. Closing Bab el-Mandeb removes this safety valve — effectively doubling the supply disruption.

## 3. 📅 Section 122 Tariff Expiry — July 24, 2026 (4 Days)

The 10-15% global import surcharge imposed under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 expires by statute on **July 24, 2026** — just 4 days away. Key update:

- The 150-day limit (since Feb 24, 2026) is a **hard statutory deadline** — the President cannot unilaterally extend
- No extension bill has been introduced in Congress
- The June 11 Federal Circuit stay keeps the tariff in force pending appeal, but does NOT override the statutory expiration
- Three scenarios: 1) Congressional extension (unlikely), 2) Replacement via Section 301/232 (some risk), 3) **Lapse with no replacement (most likely at this point)**

**Products most affected by a lapse:** Consumer electronics (smartphones, laptops), apparel & footwear, home goods, furniture, food products from China, Vietnam, India, Bangladesh.
