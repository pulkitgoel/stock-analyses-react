**Major policy day for India: Cabinet approves Semicon 2.0 with ₹1,27,500 crore outlay to deepen India's semiconductor design and manufacturing ecosystem across six pillars. Separately, Cabinet clears Mobile Phone Manufacturing Scheme (MPMS) with ₹62,500 crore budget to replace the earlier PLI scheme. PM to flag off India's first hydrogen train on July 17.**

## 1. Semicon 2.0 — ₹1,27,500 Crore Semiconductor Push

The Union Cabinet chaired by PM Modi approved **Semicon 2.0**, building on ISM 1.0, with a total outlay of **₹1,27,500 crore**. The programme targets six pillars:

1. **Design:** Deepening chip design ecosystem — 105 startups already developing chips; aim to make India a key chip design IP country
2. **Machines & Materials:** Incentivizing manufacturing of semiconductor equipment, chemicals, and gases
3. **More Fabs:** Attracting silicon fabs, compound semiconductor fabs, display fabs — first fab scheduled for 2028
4. **ATMP/OSAT:** Strengthening advanced packaging ecosystem — Micron, Kaynes, CG Semi already in commercial production
5. **R&D:** Developing nodes beyond current 28nm-110nm through domestic/international collaboration
6. **Talent:** 315 universities training 68,000+ students on chip design

Under ISM 1.0, 12 manufacturing units approved with cumulative investment of ₹1.64 lakh crore. Three companies (Micron, Kaynes, CG Semi) already started commercial production.

## 2. Mobile Phone Manufacturing Scheme (MPMS) — ₹62,500 Crore

Cabinet approved **Mobile Phone Manufacturing Scheme (MPMS)** with ₹62,500 crore outlay, replacing the earlier PLI scheme (ended March 31, 2026). The five-year scheme (FY27-FY31) targets:

- Incentives of 2.25% to 5% on eligible sales
- Additional benefits for domestic component sourcing and design/R&D
- **Cumulative production target: ₹39 lakh crore**
- **Export target: ₹15 lakh crore**
- **~60,000 direct jobs expected**
- India already 2nd largest mobile phone producer globally (behind China)
- Smartphones became India's #1 export product by value in 2025 (₹2.62 lakh crore exports)

## 3. India's First Hydrogen Train — PM to Flag Off July 17

PM Modi will flag off India's **first hydrogen-powered train** on July 17 from Jind, Haryana on the 89-km Jind-Sonipat route. Built by Indian Railways with ₹89 crore investment, the fuel cell train makes India the **3rd nation** with hydrogen train technology. PM will also inaugurate the redeveloped Jind railway station.
