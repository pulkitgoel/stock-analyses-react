## 1. EU-US Trade Deal — 15% Ceiling Now in Effect (Since July 1)

The EU-US trade deal took effect **July 1**, with most EU-origin goods now facing a **15% all-inclusive US tariff ceiling**, replacing the 10% Section 122 rate. Key details:

- 15% does NOT stack with MFN — goods with MFN rates ≥15% pay MFN only
- EU autos: 27.5% down to 15%
- EU steel and aluminum stay at 50% Section 232
- EU simultaneously eliminated all duties on US industrial goods
- US auto exports to EU: 10% to 0% (effective immediately)
- From September 1: aircraft parts, cork, generic pharma go to MFN-only

## 2. Section 301 Deadline July 20 — Potential New 12.5% Duties on 46 Countries

USTR faces a **July 20 deadline** to complete Section 301 investigations that could propose **12.5% duties on 46 countries**. This is the administration's planned replacement framework for Section 122 (which expires July 24). The countries under investigation span Asia, Europe, and Latin America.

If enacted, these country-specific 301 duties would create a complex multi-tier tariff system layered on top of Section 232 (steel/aluminum/autos) and the EU deal's 15% ceiling.
