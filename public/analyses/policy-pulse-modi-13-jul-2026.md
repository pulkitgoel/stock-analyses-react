## 🇮🇳 India Holds Out for Better Terms in US Trade Talks — Risk of Higher Tariffs Later This Month

India has **rejected a rushed trade agreement** with the US and is holding out for better terms, as PM Narendra Modi draws confidence from new trading partners, eased economic risks, and political gains at home, according to a **Reuters report published July 13**.

**What happened:**
- Months of talks failed to produce an interim trade pact during USTR Jamieson Greer's visit to New Delhi last month
- India demands: **(1)** tariff advantage over competitors like China, **(2)** guarantee against new US levies after the deal
- India refuses to cede ground on agriculture — a red line
- Trade Minister **Piyush Goyal** dismissed the Reuters report as "completely false, baseless and misleading" — but confirmed India wants a "clear advantage" from any deal

**Why India is emboldened:**
1. **US Supreme Court struck down Trump's sweeping 50% tariffs** in February 2026 — legal uncertainty favors India
2. **India's exports rose ~15%** in Apr-Jun despite Iran war disruptions
3. **UK FTA takes effect July 15**; **EU agreement** expected early 2027 — reducing dependence on US market
4. **Goldman Sachs raised India's 2026 growth forecast to 6.8%**
5. **Modi's recent state election victories** strengthen political hand
6. **Weaker rupee** improves export competitiveness
7. **22 Democratic state AGs** filed objections to Trump's proposed forced labor tariffs — India betting some US measures face legal setbacks

**The risk:** The Trump administration is expected to introduce **steeper tariffs later this month** through excess industrial capacity probes. India has denied the allegations. Washington has also proposed **12.5% additional tariffs on 44 countries** (incl. India) over forced labor allegations.

## 🇮🇳 HAL Receives 7th GE Engine, 6th Cleared — Tejas Mk1A Programme Gains Momentum

**Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL)** has received the **seventh GE F404-IN20 engine** from GE Aerospace for the Tejas Mk1A fighter, and the **sixth engine** — which had a minor technical issue — has been cleared for operational use after rectification.

**Key details:**
- 6th engine developed a snag during post-delivery inspection; GE Aerospace corrected it; HAL verified fitness
- 7th engine has now been delivered to HAL
- 99 engines contracted under ₹5,375 Cr deal with GE (2021)
- Tejas Mk1A programme remains **>2 years behind schedule**; MoD has warned HAL about financial penalties
- First batch of Mk1A fighters could be delivered **later this year** after IAF acceptance
- High-level review involving **Defence Minister Rajnath Singh**, Air Chief Marshal, and HAL CMD expected soon
