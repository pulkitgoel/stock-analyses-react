## 📈 The Bull Case (Why HOLD)

1. **Discount to peers will compress** — Even trading at 42x P/E (still below ICICI's 49x), the stock would be worth ₹633 — already above the expected listing. At 45x, it's ₹678. Over 3-6 months, as the market digests the stock, the discount should narrow.

2. **QIB 140x speaks volumes** — Institutional investors don't subscribe 140x for a flip. They see long-term compounding at a fair price.

3. **Structural tailwind** — India's MF AUM has grown at ~18-20% CAGR. SBI, with 20,000+ bank branches as distribution, is the best proxy for this trend.

4. **GMP is modest at 16%** — No crazy FOMO pricing. The listing gain is reasonable, meaning less risk of a "pop and dump."

5. **Sovereign wealth funds as anchors** — GIC, ADIA — these are patient capital. They aren't selling on day one.

---

## 📉 The Bear Case (Why SELL)

1. **Lower yield per AUM** — SBI earns less per ₹1 Cr of AUM compared to HDFC/ICICI. The P/E discount may be structural, not temporary.

2. **OFS = selling pressure** — SBI and Amundi are offloading shares. While this is priced in, any further selling by the parent could weigh on the stock.

3. **AMC stocks are momentum-driven** — HDFC AMC and ICICI AMC have had a strong run. A market correction could hit AMC stocks hard due to their correlation with equity markets.

4. **Passive investing threat** — As index funds and ETFs grow, active management fee margins compress. SBI has a large passive book, which is good for AUM but bad for fee income.

5. **Lock-in expiry** — Anchor investors (129 entities) have a 30-day lock-in. If they sell after that, it could create supply pressure.
